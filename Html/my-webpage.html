Historical Places in Cape Town

City Hall

  • Cape Town City Hall is a large Edwardian building in Cape Town city centre.
  • It was on the same spot where he made a speech when he was released from prison on 11 February 1990.
  • On 24 July 2018, a statue of Nelson Mandela on the balcony overlooking the Grand Parade was unveiled.

Distirct Six Museum

  • District Six before its destruction under Apartheid, was a community representative of diversity across the spectrum.
  • The Museum which was launched on 10 December 1994.
  • District Six represented the polar opposite of what the Apartheid government needed people to believe and internalise.

Castle of Good Hope

  • The Castle of Good Hope is known locally as "The Castle".
  • The Castle was declared a historical monument (now a provincial heritage site) in 1936.
  • It is considered the best preserved example of a 17th century architectural structure in the entire world.
